Young Artists Studio

Creative Foundations in Art & Artists

Ages 4–6 · 45 minutes · San Diego

Young Artists Studio is an early childhood art program at Curious Studio where children ages 4–6 explore famous artists through hands-on, open-ended art. In a thoughtfully designed studio environment, children build creative confidence while strengthening focus, fine motor skills, communication, and early problem-solving — all through joyful making.

Who This Class Is For

This class is designed for children who:

  • Can comfortably hold and use crayons, colored pencils, and paint brushes
  • Do not put art materials in their mouth (crayons, paint, brushes, etc.)
  • Are fully potty trained and able to wash hands independently
  • Are typically between 4–6 years old, with the class structured around the average developmental range of this age group

Drop-off class
Parents are welcome to stay and observe or participate if they prefer, but children are encouraged to experience the class independently.

What We Do in Class

Each session follows a predictable, child-friendly rhythm that supports focus, independence, and confidence:

Class Flow (45 minutes):

Hello & welcome → Artist of the day → Create → Share & connect → Clean up

Artist of the Day

  • Children are introduced to one artist per class through simple, age-appropriate discussion and visuals
  • We explore how artists use color, shapes, lines, textures, or patterns — no art history lectures, just inspiration

Create & Explore

  • Children create their own artwork inspired by the artist's style
  • Emphasis is on exploration, decision-making, and choice, not copying or perfection
  • Every child's artwork looks different — by design

Share & Celebrate

  • At the end of class, children post their artwork on our gallery wall
  • When parents arrive, children are invited to introduce their work, describe their ideas, and practice speaking in front of others

Week-Long Program Experience

For students enrolled in a 5-day program:

  • Each day features a different artist and artistic approach
  • On Day 5, children create a final piece using any styles they loved most
  • Each child selects one favorite artwork to be professionally framed
  • All artwork created during the week is sent home

Our Teaching Principles & Practices

Our program is thoughtfully designed based on early childhood development research and best practices.

  • Process over product: We focus on exploration and creative thinking rather than producing identical artwork.
  • Small class sizes: Low child-to-teacher ratios allow for individualized guidance, emotional support, and personalization.
  • Social learning: Art becomes a natural setting to practice turn-taking, communication, collaboration, and respect for others' ideas.
  • Growing independence: Children practice following multi-step directions, making choices, managing materials, and participating in cleanup.
  • Age-appropriate structure: Short, predictable blocks with a clear routine help children feel safe, focused, and successful.

Why Art Matters (Ages 4–6)

For young children, art isn't just fun — it supports core development and school readiness:

  • Fine Motor Skills: Strengthens hand muscles and coordination needed for writing and everyday self-care
  • Language & Expression: Builds vocabulary, storytelling, and confidence through describing ideas and artwork
  • Emotional Regulation & Confidence: Supports persistence, flexibility, and pride in one's work
  • Cognitive & School-Readiness Skills: Encourages attention, planning, sequencing, problem-solving, and flexible thinking

These benefits are supported by trusted early childhood education research (NAEYC, CDC, and early learning frameworks), which emphasize creative play and process-based art as essential to healthy development.

Inventor Studio

Thinking Through Making

Ages 7–10 · 50 minutes · San Diego

Inventor Studio is a small-group lab where children learn to turn ideas into purposeful creations. Through questioning, sketching, and prototyping, students explore how objects and systems are designed — and build thoughtful solutions of their own.

Who This Class Is For

Inventor Studio is designed for children who:

  • Can follow multi-step directions independently
  • Can use scissors and basic tools safely
  • Enjoy asking "why" and thinking about how things work
  • Are typically between 7–10 years old, with the class structured around the developmental range of this age group

Reading and writing skills vary at this age, and that's completely normal. Instructions are provided verbally and visually, and students are supported in expressing ideas in ways that work best for them.

How the Program Works

Inventor Studio includes two complementary tracks:

  • Object Lab — Designing tangible, everyday objects
  • Systems Lab — Designing rules, structures, and invisible systems

Both tracks follow a structured creative process:

Observe → Question → Define Purpose → Sketch → Prototype → Share → Clean

Students are guided step-by-step through this process, building independence and confidence along the way.

Track 1: Object Lab

Designing Everyday Things

In Object Lab, students examine familiar objects and rethink their function, form, and purpose.

What Happens in Class

Each session focuses on one everyday object — such as a kettle, chair, spoon, bag, or clock.

Students:

  1. Observe & Interact — Handle and analyze the object, identify how it works, discuss the problem it solves
  2. Define Purpose — What need does this object address? Who is it designed for? What constraints or trade-offs exist?
  3. Ideate & Sketch — Generate multiple ideas, sketch redesigned versions, add written notes explaining features and purpose
  4. Prototype — Build a functional or structural model using materials such as cardboard, clay, paper, fabric, and found objects
  5. Share — Explain their thinking, describe the problem they aimed to solve, discuss possible improvements

The focus is clarity of thinking — not aesthetic perfection.

Track 2: Systems Lab

Designing Rules & Structures

In Systems Lab, students design intangible systems — the invisible frameworks that shape how we live, move, and interact.

What Happens in Class

Each session explores a system such as: Traffic light systems, Timers, Board game rules, Birthday party schedules, Trip planning structures, App concepts

Students:

  1. Analyze the System — What rules make this work? How do people interact with it? What happens if something changes?
  2. Identify Goals — What is this system trying to accomplish? Who benefits from it? What challenges might exist?
  3. Design Their Own Version — Modify or redesign the system, clarify new rules and structure
  4. Map the Logic — Diagram how it works step-by-step, identify cause-and-effect relationships
  5. Prototype & Share — Create visual models, flowcharts, rule cards, or mock interfaces; explain reasoning and design choices

Systems Lab strengthens structured reasoning and multi-step thinking in a concrete, age-appropriate way.

Why This Matters

At ages 7–10, children are developing stronger logical reasoning and structured thinking. Inventor Studio channels that growth into purposeful creativity.

  • Purpose-driven thinking — identifying real problems and defining clear goals
  • Logical reasoning — thinking step-by-step and understanding cause and effect
  • Executive function — planning, organizing, and following through
  • Creative confidence — turning ideas into tangible solutions
  • Communication skills — explaining and defending their thinking

In a world increasingly shaped by automation, the ability to define problems and design thoughtful solutions remains essential. Inventor Studio nurtures those capacities — through making.

About Curious Studio

We believe children learn best when they feel curious, capable, and supported. At Curious Studio, we create space for children to ask questions, experiment, take creative risks, and learn through the process of making—not by aiming for perfect results. Art, design, and problem-solving are at the heart of our work, shaping how we invite children to explore ideas and their own creativity.

Curious Studio was founded by Nuri Kim, inspired by her experiences designing new technologies, solving complex problems, and raising a young child. With over 15 years of experience in product design at companies including Uber and Alphabet, along with a background in education and teaching, Nuri brings a thoughtful, human-centered approach to how children learn and create. She holds an MFA from the California College of the Arts and a BA in Education from Korea University.

Curious Studio is also shaped by Sonal Jain, our Chief Math Officer. He holds a PhD in Mathematics from Harvard University and has worked as a mathematician across academic and applied settings for nearly two decades. A proud alumnus of a high school math camp in Michigan, Sonal developed his deep love for mathematics through exploration, curiosity, and problem-solving—values that continue to shape Curious Studio today.

Together, they created Curious Studio as a creative space where children nurture curiosity, build confidence, and cultivate a joy of making.
